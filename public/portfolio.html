Teaching Electronics

Electronics Workshops

In early December, I led a Workshop on analog electronics and electrical engineering with a special focus on audio amplification. Nine attendees assembled their very own small amplifier kit, which was designed using Autodesk Eagle CAD and based around the ubiquitous LM386 Operational Amplifier. The workshop took place in the Wond’ry Makerspace at Vanderbilt University, and was funded by the Wond’ry.

Music + Electronics

Amplifier Project

Built from two Goodwill-sourced amplifiers from the 1980s, this project modernizes old hi-fi audio technology via Bluetooth connectivity, circuit and load protection, and improved heat dissipation. What you see here are two 150 Watt-per-channel class AB amplifiers that share a case made from plywood. The LED-lined case is finished with clear acrylic that encloses the electronics. The finished amplifier is also fitted with hidden external potentiometers that allow the user to set a volume limit. The finished project was featured at the 2019 Nashville Makerfaire held at Vanderbilt University.
